The most misdiagnosed condition in women over 40? Tendinopathy

Tendinopathy refers to the breakdown of tendons – and it’s the secret culprit behind most “joint pain” cases in women over 40. Unfortunately, most doctors quickly write it off as “arthritis” without doing in-depth tests. 

That’s a big mistake – because tendinopathy usually occurs BEFORE arthritis. Joints only start to wear and tear when connective tissues aren’t strong enough to handle the stress.

Simply put: Nipping tendinopathy in the bud could save your joints.

Here’s what most doctors get wrong:

It’s not a simple “inflammation” problem, it’s collagen degeneration

Reaching overhead feels like the shoulder's ripping apart?

Probably Rotator Cuff Tendinopathy - micro-tears in the rotator cuff tendons.

Can't sleep on your side without hips screaming?

Could be Gluteal Tendinopathy - breakdown of tendons in the hips and buttocks.

Knees hurt when bending?

Chances are it's Patellar Tendinopathy - breakdown of the patellar tendon, which connects the kneecap to the shinbone.

First few steps in the morning feel like walking on broken glass?

9 times out of 10, it's Plantar Fasciitis - a common type of foot tendinopathy
